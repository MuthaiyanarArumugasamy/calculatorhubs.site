What is BMI? Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple method used to estimate whether your body weight is healthy for your height. It is widely used by doctors, hospitals, fitness trainers, and health organizations around the world. BMI does not directly measure body fat, but it provides a quick indication of whether a person is underweight, at a healthy weight, overweight, or obese. How is BMI Calculated? The BMI formula is: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²) Example Weight = 70 kg Height = 1.75 m BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) BMI = 22.86 This BMI falls in the healthy weight category. It does not consider: Muscle mass Bone density Age Gender Body fat distribution For example, athletes may have a high BMI because of muscle mass, not excess body fat. How to Maintain a Healthy BMI Eat a Balanced Diet Include: Fruits Vegetables Whole grains Lean protein Healthy fats Avoid excessive sugar and processed foods. Exercise Regularly Aim for at least: 150 minutes of moderate exercise every week Examples: Walking Running Cycling Swimming Yoga Drink Plenty of Water Water helps improve digestion and supports healthy metabolism. Adults should generally drink enough fluids throughout the day based on their activity level and climate. Get Enough Sleep Adults should aim for around 7–9 hours of quality sleep each night. Good sleep supports overall health and helps with weight management. Reduce Stress Stress may affect eating habits and physical activity. Try: Meditation Deep breathing Walking Reading books Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) Is BMI accurate? BMI is a useful screening tool but does not directly measure body fat. Can children use BMI? Children and teenagers use BMI-for-age charts rather than adult BMI categories. Is BMI useful for athletes? Athletes may have a higher BMI because of greater muscle mass, so BMI alone may not reflect their health status. How often should I calculate BMI? Checking once every month is generally sufficient unless advised otherwise by a healthcare professional. Conclusion BMI is a simple and effective way to understand whether your weight is generally appropriate for your height. While it is not a complete measure of health, it can be a useful starting point when combined with healthy eating, regular exercise, good sleep, and professional medical advice when needed.
